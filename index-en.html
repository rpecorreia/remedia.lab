An experienced American journalist in the midst of about two dozen regional media journalists from the center, to reflect on “The elements of journalism in the new digital ecosystem”. This was the training cycle led by Walter Dean, which ran from September 6 to December 20, 2019.


The importance of journalism and the way journalists do their work were at the heart of the initiative, promoted by Re/media.Lab. Very much based on practical cases and the debate among all the participants, this cycle was well received and participated. In the end, the balance was positive and the possibility of repeating the training in the future remained open.




The importance of local journalism

The characteristics that a journalist must have, the differences between exercise journalism in the USA and Europe and the importance of local journalism, were some of the topics addressed by Walter Dean, in an interview he gave to the participants, at the end of the last session. We then share the result of about 30 minutes of conversation.

A set of 15 measures for the media sector in Portugal was presented at the Faculty of Arts and Letters of the University of Beira Interior (UBI), on November 25, 2019. An initiative of Re/media.Lab, which for this purpose convened a press conference, attended by journalists and students.



"How am I going to think about my future, if I don't know what's going on in my country, in the community where I live?" The question was raised by João Carlos Correia, researcher in charge of Re/media.Lab and professor at UBI, during the press conference.



"They [regional media] are important. They are what give visibility to the party, to the club, to our problems", he reinforced, to justify that the moment is opportune for the initiative. It is a response by a group of professors and researchers at UBI - the overwhelming majority of Re/media.Lab - to the growing apprehension around the crisis situation of national and regional journalism, which culminated in a new and recent statement by the President of the Republic, on the need to include support measures for social communication in the State Budget for 2020. This concern should be extended to regional media, whose contribution is fundamental to the increase of media literacy and to the fight against disinformation in regions of low density, interior and peripheral or close to less favored extracts.



Business models and media financing, as well as the role of the Regional Development and Coordination Commissions (CCDR’s), were topics covered. "At a recent meeting requested by the Portuguese Press Association, which wanted to hear from us about regional newspapers, we learned that the role of CCDR’s is extremely relevant. The more active they are in informing and supporting the regional media in formalizing applications, perhaps more means and more support may be considered", he underlined.




Measures proposed by Re/media.Lab

  1. Offer all young people who reach the age of 18 or students, as well as the senior population, an annual subscription to a generalist newspaper (national or local) of their choice (paper or online). It can be associated with student and senior cards;
  2. To oblige all public bodies (from national to local) serving the citizen to make two generalist newspapers (national and local) available in waiting spaces (paper and online);
  3. Include in the public transport concession contracts the obligation of the concessionaire to make general paper newspapers available on buses/coaches and/or wi-fi with free access to newspaper sites;
  4. Distribute 10% of the tourist tax for each city to local newspapers;
  5. Create an annual “Journalism Contribution” fee (identical to the Audiovisual Contribution on the EDP invoice), to be paid by all Internet Package subscribers. One can also think of its channeling for postal distribution;
  6. Amend the Law on Private Copying, increasing and sharing the rate applied to smartphones and tablets with the communication companies;
  7. Increase the postage paid, with an increase for the Interior and peripheral regions, according to objective criteria to be established (capitation, capitation of reading, etc.);
  8. Deduct, at the tax level, those who buy/subscribe subscriptions;
  9. Encourage quality with several sub-measures;
  10. Include media literacy and media education modules in the education system, in an informal and classroom context, in which newspapers (national and local) appear. Associated with this issue, promote the creation of school newspapers;
  11. Create collectives that exercise a function of ombudsman and observatory, bringing together journalists and citizens;
  12. Create measures to protect rights, with respect to ownership and sharing of journalistic content on social networks;
  13. Putting information in fashion (well-made, attractive campaigns, with content and on platforms suitable for the target audience);
  14. Introduce measures to support resource sharing by regional media, through collaborative and cooperative mechanisms;
  15. Rethink and relaunch the Regional Press Portal, with the aim of having these media online and accessible through mobile devices. This is a measure that aims to allow all regional and local media that wish, the possibility of being accessible in the digital context.


In addition to the measures presented, João Carlos Correia also advanced that attention to the sector and the response of the project he leads will continue, having announced the realization of two events for the first half of 2020: a conference on financing of regional media, with responsible for European and national fund management, as well as a spring school (Local Media Spring School), eminently practical and dedicated to editorial design.

"O pulsar da proximidade nos media e no jornalismo" is the title of the book that results from a selection of studies presented during the Local Media Fal{l} School (19-21 September 2019) and recently launched.

"Research around the media, whether regional, local, hyperlocal or even community, has aroused growing interest in recent years by the academic community. After a period of speeches about globalization, boosted by technological development, but above all by the appearance of the Internet, the trend now seems to be to call for a return to the place. At the heart of this return is the recognition of the importance of (re) visiting territories and communities and (re) discovering identities. For that, it is necessary to stop, look and look again, to dialogue, to know. And read ", write the editors Pedro Jerónimo and João Carlos Correia in the introduction of the book.

The book is accessible for free through the LabCom website, a research unit where Re/media.Lab is integrated.
